D3 Visualization

Description

The following bubble chart presents project management information for the 15 executive departments of the United States administration, between the period of 2006 to 2012.

Each bubble in the chart represents a department, labeled by its official abbreviation. Departments are positioned according to their Average Cost Variance percentage (x-axis), and their Average Schedule Variance percentage (y-axis), for all the projects they started within the above mentioned period. According to their positioning each department falls in one of four distinct categories:

  • In Time - In Budget: Departments that on average complete projects ahead of schedule and within their initial budget.
  • Late - In Budget: Departments that on average complete projects behind schedule but within their initial budget.
  • Late - Out of Budget: Departments that on average complete projects behind schedule and excede their initial budget.
  • In Time - Out of Budget: Departments that on average complete projects ahead of schedule but excede their initial budget.

The area of each bubble is proportioned to the total number of projects carried out by that department. The larger the bubble is the more projects this department had initiated between 2006-2012. The color of each bubble, ranging from light purple to a darker shade, represents the average project cost in millions for each department. The darker a bubble is the higher their average project cost.

The legend, on the side of the chart, contains a list of all the departments in the visualisation. On the left of the department's name there is a small circle colored as the corresponding bubble in the chart. On the right side of the department's name there is the number of initiated projects, on top of a blue bar that visualises this number. The departments in the legend appear in ascending order according the their total number of projects.

Interactivity

Hovering with the mouse over a bubble in the chart, will cause the bubble to grow in size and to turn opaque. A tooltip box will appear to the right side of the bubble, containing the following information:

  • The complete official name of the department
  • The total number of projects it initiated between 2006-2012
  • A color scale, with an animated index, which indicates the average project cost of that particular department in million dollars, and how that relates to the lowest and highest average project cost among all other departments.

Hovering over the items in the legend, makes them to highlight, while the corresponding bubble in the chart grows in size and turns opaque.

Audience

This visualisation is intended for the United States administration, and aims to help them identify the executive departments that on average excede their initial schedule and budget when carrying out a project. Based on the results of the current analysis and visualisation, emerges the need for a more focused analysis that will investigate the reasons behind the innefieciency of particular departments.

Information on the average project cost and number of projects per departent, that are also presented in the visualisation, are useful in order to help the administration decide in which of the innefficient departments the problem is more urgent.

For example, the Department of Defense, is positioned close to the Department of Education, in the "Late & Out of Budget" quarter of the graph, however the issue is significantly more serious in the Department of Defense, since on average each project costs 20.26 million dollars, with a total number of 66 projects, whereas in the Department of Education the average project cost is only 1.63 millions, with a total number of 26 projects. Arguably, it is more urgent to tackle the innefieciency in the Department of Defense because it introduces higher absolute costs.
